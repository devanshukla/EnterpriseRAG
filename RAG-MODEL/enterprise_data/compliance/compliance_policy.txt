ACME CORP — COMPLIANCE & REGULATORY POLICY v3.1 (CONFIDENTIAL)
=================================================================
Effective: January 1, 2025 | Owner: Eve Martinez, Compliance Officer

SECTION 1: DATA PROTECTION COMPLIANCE (GDPR / CCPA)
----------------------------------------
All personal employee and customer data must be encrypted at rest (AES-256)
and in transit (TLS 1.3 minimum).
Data retention schedules:
  - Financial records: 7 years minimum
  - HR/employee records: 5 years after separation
  - Audit logs: 3 years minimum
  - Customer communications: 2 years
Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) must be fulfilled within 30 days.
Right to erasure requests must be assessed within 72 hours.

SECTION 2: ACCESS CONTROL POLICY
----------------------------------------
Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) is enforced across all internal systems.
Privileged access (admin-level) requires Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
Access reviews are conducted quarterly — unused accounts deactivated within 30 days.
Contractor accounts must be deactivated within 24 hours of engagement end.
Password policy: minimum 12 characters, rotation every 90 days, no reuse of last 10.

SECTION 3: AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------------------
All system access, data modifications, and permission changes must be logged.
Audit logs must be immutable and stored in a tamper-evident system.
Internal audit: quarterly by Compliance team.
External audit: annual by Big 4 certified auditors (next: November 2025).
SOC 2 Type II certification renewal: December 2025.

SECTION 4: INCIDENT RESPONSE PLAN
----------------------------------------
Data breaches must be reported to regulatory authorities within 72 hours (GDPR Art. 33).
Incident response team notification: within 1 hour of detection.
Containment target: within 4 hours of confirmed breach.
Post-incident review required within 5 business days.
Incident severity levels: P1 (Critical) → P2 (High) → P3 (Medium) → P4 (Low).

SECTION 5: VENDOR & THIRD-PARTY RISK
----------------------------------------
All vendors with data access require signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA).
Annual security questionnaire mandatory for Tier 1 vendors.
Vendor access limited to minimum necessary data (principle of least privilege).
Third-party penetration test required for any new critical vendor integrations.
